To be, or not to be: that is the question:

Whether 'tis nobler in the mind to suffer The slings and arrows of outrageos fortune, Or to take arms against a sea of troubles, And by opposing end them? To die: to sleep; No more;

To be or not to be, that is the question’ is the most famous soliloquy in the works of Shakespeare
– quite possibly the most famous soliloquy in literature.

Hamlet is thinking about life and death. It is the great question that Hamlet is asking about human existence in general and his own existence in particular – a reflection on whether it’s better to be alive or to be dead.
